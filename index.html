WHAT MAKES A MAN?

Men, Masculinity And Mental Health

Traditional gender roles have a decisive influence on how we relate to others and to ourselves. Male role expectations have been shown to negatively affect men's mental health. Strong, self-reliant, confident, dominant and courageous are adjectives that define what a man should be. The insatiable quest of men to fit the male stereotype cuses mental overload and often prevents men from talking about their weaknesses, emotions, feelings and asking for the necessary psychological help.

Strong, Self-Reliant, Confident But Sometimes Weak With Emotions And Feelings.

I am a very emotional person and a lot of small things that come from friends and family affect me. However, I am blunt and honest most of the time, and whenever I face issues or feel down, I vent to my friends. I think men should start reaching out to people willing to listen and can understand without judgement.

Sometimes I'm playful and I don't think I'm alpha. But when the time comes, I am quite aggressive, strong, and dependable. Society always demands people to be what they are not. It's 2022, It's time we [men] should stop falling into other people's perception of masculinity and start being ourselves.

Being a man is not about having balls; that's just biology! For me, being a man is about self-discovery, building self-awareness, emotional intelligence and most importantly, looking out for each other

In my country, Nigeria, when you are vulnerable as a man, you get to hear phrases like man up or men shouldn't be emotional. Cmon! A man is also a human being; every human being has emotion. My message to every man out there is that it is okay not to be okay. Open up to someone who genuinely cares about you.

I think it's different. I think everyone has their different ways of viewing being a man. Personally, I think what it takes... Actually, I don't think it takes anything really, it's just who you are what you are. I personally don't see men as people having to be strong, having to be this or something to be that.

I think we all need to start listening to each other; not hearing but listening. We don’t need to be a mental health expert to make a difference to someone who is struggling. Just start by listening, men and women, both!
